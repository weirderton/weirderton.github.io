Speed Painting

4/4/2016

 
Digital Speed painting are for digital artist who loves just to sketch but wants to create something that is still beautiful. In this case, most of my digital speed arts are landscape type because i find it easy to paint landscape scenes. Landscape scenes are are not that detail that's why you can easily paint them. Here are some of the guidelines in speed painting.
  1. Think of a landscape scene where you can do some shadow drawing. e.g. Couple in the beach during the sunset.
  2. Start with a gradient background appropriate to the scene.
  3. Sketch the scene using a single color brush, having a 50 % flow. and 75% opacity.
  4. Paint the shadow parts or also known as the dark parts.
  5. Then, put some highlights and some little detail to the scene.
  6. Use a colors appropriately.
  7. Lastly, overlay a gradient that is appropriate to your scene.

Character Parts & Phonemes

4/1/2016

 
In animation of characters, we have so called the phonemes. Phonemes is the animation of the face of your character talking. It requires you to create different poses of your character saying some of the significant letters. Also, is not just the mouth where animation rather also on the jaws eye brows and the like. This will make your character phonemes more realistic and convincing. In the above picture, that is my character, breakdown into its parts so that on the part of creating the poses you will not have difficulties.

Steps in breaking down the parts of your character.
  1. Create your character parts in every layer.
  2. Avoid merging layers that will technically combined two parts of the character.
  3. Save the png files of every part.

Steps in creating your phonemes.
  1. First choose a part of your character where you want to start. e.g. mouth
  2. Then, create 8 poses of that part. Those 8 poses can be composed of the 5 vowels, f letter, l letter, and m letter. But if you think that it is appropriate or enough for the character, add or modify any pose you want.
  3. Lastly, save the png files of every letter or pose of your character.
